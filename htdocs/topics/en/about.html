About Cramwords

Cramwords is a non-profit word-learning service in the Internet.

The goal of Cramwords is to suggest an easy-to-use environment that allows everyone to teach and learn foreign words, including general vocabulary, technical terms, slang, and more. With Cramwords each teacher or language school can assemble vocabularies in line with their syllabus and share them with their students.

Cramwords aims to give students not only a word memorization training apparatus but also a tool helping them bring the knowledge into a system. We divide the words into functional groups, and each reflects a particular subject domain or an abstract relation.

A subject domain is a set of things, properties, and actions possible in the real world. Animals, books, food, and so on are subject domains.

An abstract relation expresses a speaker's point of view on a subject domain. Say, we can prefer cats to dogs, Shakespeare to Chaucer, or pizza to steak (and vice versa if you like so more!). We can place subjects in space and order actions in time. We can use the exact words to speak about our possibilities, intentions, and duties regarding different subject domains.

To make a long story short, we divide a vocabulary into mental material and a mental toolbox. The material should be enough for speaker aims, while the toolbox should be full-featured.

We have the following features in Cramwords now.

For teachers For students
  • Using our templates for creating dictionaries
  • Assigning multilingual translations to a word
  • Declaring functional groups by defining tags
  • Putting words to functional groups using tags
  • Giving a mnemonic phrase to each word
  • Linking words to external online dictionaries
  • Publishing vocabularies in the Internet
  • Working with vocabularies on the Cramwords web site
  • Selecting words by multiple functional groups
  • Cramming selected words using translations and mnemos
  • Viewing selected words and browsing to dictionaries
  • Formatting seleted words to cards for printing them
